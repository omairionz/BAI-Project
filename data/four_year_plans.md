# Example 4-Year Plans — UVA Computer Science

These are example semester-by-semester schedules for BACS and BSCS students. Actual schedules will vary based on AP/IB credit, transfer credit, and individual choices. Always confirm your plan with your CS advisor.

---

## BSCS Example 4-Year Plan

The BSCS is offered through the School of Engineering and Applied Science (SEAS). Students must satisfy both SEAS general requirements and CS-specific requirements.

### Year 1 — Fall Semester
- CS 1110 - Introduction to Programming (3 credits)
- APMA 1110 - Single Variable Calculus II (4 credits)
- CHEM 1410 - Introductory College Chemistry I (3 credits)
- CHEM 1411 - Introductory Chemistry I Lab (1 credit)
- ENGR 1010 - Engineering Foundations 1 (4 credits)
- **Total: ~15 credits**

### Year 1 — Spring Semester
- CS 2100 - Data Structures and Algorithms 1 (4 credits)
- APMA 2120 - Multivariable Calculus (4 credits)
- PHYS 1425 - Introductory Physics 1 for Engineers (3 credits)
- PHYS 1429 - Introductory Physics 1 Workshop (1 credit)
- ENGR 1020 - Engineering Foundations 2 (3 credits)
- **Total: ~15 credits**

### Year 2 — Fall Semester
- CS 2120 - Discrete Mathematics and Theory 1 (3 credits)
- CS 2130 - Computer Systems and Organization 1 (4 credits)
- Physics 2 and Lab: ECE 2200 OR PHYS 2415 + PHYS 2419 (4 credits)
- STS 2600 - Engineering Ethics (3 credits)
- HSS Elective (3 credits)
- **Total: ~17 credits**

### Year 2 — Spring Semester
- CS 3100 - Data Structures and Algorithms 2 (3 credits)
- CS 3130 - Computer Systems and Organization 2 (4 credits)
- APMA 3100 - Probability (3 credits)
- Math/Science Elective (3 credits)
- HSS Elective (3 credits)
- **Total: ~16 credits**

### Year 3 — Fall Semester
- CS 3120 - Discrete Mathematics and Theory 2 (3 credits)
- CS 3140 - Software Development Essentials (3 credits)
- CS 3240 - Software Engineering (3 credits)
- APMA Elective — choose from APMA 2130, 3080, 3120, or 3150 (3-4 credits)
- Unrestricted Elective (3 credits)
- **Total: ~15-16 credits**

### Year 3 — Spring Semester
- CS Elective 1 (3 credits)
- CS Elective 2 (3 credits)
- APMA Elective — second choice from approved list (3 credits)
- HSS Elective (3 credits)
- Unrestricted Elective (3 credits)
- **Total: ~15 credits**

### Year 4 — Fall Semester
- CS Elective 3 (3 credits)
- CS Elective 4 (3 credits)
- CS Capstone — CS 4980, CS 4970, or CS 4991 option (3 credits)
- STS 4500 - STS and Engineering Practice (3 credits)
- Unrestricted Elective (3 credits)
- **Total: ~15 credits**

### Year 4 — Spring Semester

- STS 4600 - The Engineer, Ethics, and Professional Responsibility (3 credits)

- Unrestricted Elective (3 credits)

## BACS Example 4-Year Plan

The BACS is offered through the College of Arts & Sciences. Students follow College general education requirements instead of SEAS engineering requirements. Students must complete CS 111X and CS 2100 before declaring the major.

### Year 1 — Fall Semester
- CS 1110 - Introduction to Programming (3 credits)
- College Writing Requirement Course (3 credits)
- Foreign Language (4 credits)
- Area Requirement Elective — Humanities or Social Science (3 credits)
- **Total: ~13 credits**

*Note: Students should prioritize CS 1110 and CS 2100 as early as possible, as these are required to declare the major.*

- Foreign Language continued (4 credits)
- Natural Science area requirement (3 credits)

## Tips for Planning Your Schedule

### Prerequisites matter — plan ahead
- CS 2100 must come before CS 3100 and CS 3140
- CS 2120 must come before CS 3120 and CS 3100
- CS 2130 must come before CS 3130 and CS 3100
- CS 3100 must come before CS 3120
- Don't wait too long to start the foundation sequence — it takes 2-3 semesters to complete

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Skipping CS 2130 early — it unlocks CS 3130, which is required for both degrees
- Forgetting about APMA 3100 (required for BSCS) — take it by junior year
- Leaving STS 4500/4600 for the wrong year — these are ONLY offered fall/spring of senior year for BSCS
- Stacking too many hard CS courses (CS 3100, CS 3120, CS 3130) in the same semester

### When to take hard courses
- CS 3100 (DSA 2) and CS 3130 (Systems 2) are considered the hardest foundation courses
- Avoid taking both CS 3100 and CS 3130 in the same semester if possible
- CS 3240 (Software Engineering) is project-heavy — plan for significant team project time

### AP/IB Credit
- AP Computer Science A (score 4 or 5) gives credit for CS 1110
- AP Calculus BC (score 4 or 5) may give credit for APMA 1090 and/or APMA 1110
- AP Chemistry may satisfy the CHEM 1410/1411 requirement for BSCS students
- Consult your advisor to confirm how your AP credits apply

## Choosing Between BACS and BSCS

| Factor | BACS | BSCS |
|---|---|---|
| School | College of Arts & Sciences | Engineering (SEAS) |
| Math required | College math area req | Calculus I, II, III + 3 APMA courses |
| CS electives | 9 credits (3 courses) | 15 credits (5 courses) |
| Integration electives | Required (12 credits) | Not required |
| Senior thesis | Not required | Required (STS 4500 + 4600) |
| Capstone | Not required | Required |
| GPA requirement | None for CS | 2.0 CS GPA to graduate |
| Flexibility | More free electives | More structured |

Choose **BACS** if: you want to combine CS with another field, want more schedule flexibility, are interested in tech + humanities/social science, or are already in the College.

Choose **BSCS** if: you want a more rigorous technical degree, are interested in graduate school in CS/engineering, want an ABET-accredited degree, or are already in SEAS.
