# Computer Science B.A. (BACS) - UVA

## Overview

The Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science (BACS) is offered through the **College of Arts & Sciences** at UVA. It is designed for students who want a strong foundation in computer science combined with courses in arts, humanities, and sciences, developing an understanding of how computing connects with other fields.

To enroll in the BACS major, students must be enrolled in the **College of Arts & Sciences**. Engineering students are NOT eligible for the BACS, but are welcome to pursue the BSCS or CS minor.

---

## How BACS Differs from BSCS

- BACS is in the College of Arts & Sciences; BSCS is in the School of Engineering (SEAS)
- BACS requires 3 CS elective courses (9 credits); BSCS requires 5 courses (15 credits)
- BACS requires 4 Integration Electives (12 credits); BSCS does NOT have this requirement
- BSCS requires a fourth-year senior thesis (STS 4500 + STS 4600); BACS does NOT require a thesis
- BSCS requires CS 3240 (Software Engineering) and a Capstone course; BACS does NOT
- BACS has NO minimum GPA requirement for CS courses; BSCS requires a 2.0 CS GPA to graduate
- Both degrees share the same CS foundation courses

---

## Prerequisites to Declare the BACS

Before declaring the major, students must complete:

- CS 111X - Introduction to Programming (Credits: 3) — CS 1110, 1111, 1112, 1113, or 1120
- CS 2100 - Data Structures and Algorithms 1 (Credits: 4)

Both must be completed with a **C+ or higher**. Students may also satisfy these requirements through a place-out test.

---

## College of Arts & Sciences General Requirements

BACS students follow the College's general education competency requirements, NOT the SEAS engineering requirements. The traditional College requirements include:

- Two writing requirements
- A foreign language requirement
- Area requirements in: Natural Science & Mathematics, Social Sciences, Humanities, and Historical Studies

For full details on College general education requirements, consult your Advising Dean (not the CS department — the CS department handles CS-specific questions only).

## CS Foundation Courses (20 credits)

All BACS majors must complete the following foundation courses:

- CS 2120 - Discrete Mathematics and Theory 1 (Credits: 3)
- CS 2130 - Computer Systems and Organization 1 (Credits: 4)
- CS 3100 - Data Structures and Algorithms 2 (Credits: 3)
- CS 3120 - Discrete Mathematics and Theory 2 (Credits: 3)
- CS 3130 - Computer Systems and Organization 2 (Credits: 4)
- CS 3140 - Software Development Essentials (Credits: 3)

These are the same foundation courses required for BSCS majors. The 2000-level courses should be completed before the 3000-level courses, and prerequisites must be followed carefully.

---

## CS Electives (9 credits)

BACS majors must complete **9 credits** (normally 3 courses) of CS electives.

Rules for CS electives:
- Must have the CS mnemonic
- Must be at the 3000-level or higher
- Must NOT be a foundation course listed above
- At most 3 credits of CS 4993 (Independent Study) may count
- CS 4980 and CS 4998 cannot count toward this requirement

Popular CS elective areas include: Machine Learning, Computer Vision, Cybersecurity, Computer Networks, Database Systems, and Human-Computer Interaction.

---

## Integration Electives (12 credits)

BACS majors must complete **12 credits** (normally 4 courses) of Integration Electives. This is unique to the BACS and not required for the BSCS.

Integration electives are non-CS courses that:
- Explore applications of computing to arts and sciences fields in a significant way, OR
- Provide fundamental computing depth and background
- Must be offered by departments in the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences
- Must be taken for a letter grade

The official list of approved integration electives is maintained in the UVA Undergraduate Record. The list is updated once per year (in the summer). Always check the official list in the UG Record — that is the authoritative source.

Examples of departments that offer approved integration electives include: Economics, Statistics, Linguistics, Philosophy, Psychology, Biology, Mathematics, and Political Science.

If a course is not on the official list but seems like it should qualify, students can submit a petition to the BACS Program Director.

---

## GPA Requirements

- BACS majors have **NO minimum CS GPA requirement** to graduate
- However, students must earn a C- or higher in courses that serve as prerequisites for later courses
- Note: the BSCS requires a 2.0 CS GPA to graduate, but BACS does not have this rule

---

## Distinguished Majors Program (DMP)

BACS students are not required to write a thesis, but may optionally apply to the Distinguished Majors Program (DMP).

To complete the distinguished major, students must:
- Complete all usual BACS degree requirements
- Complete a fourth-year thesis project approved by two readers
- Complete two semesters of CS 4998 - Distinguished BA Majors Research (3 credits each semester)

The BACS DMP is managed by Prof. Yixin Sun. For more information, visit: https://csdmp.github.io/

## How to Declare the BACS

1. Complete CS 111X and CS 2100 with C+ or higher
2. Go to the College's Declaration of Major form (use Chrome browser)
3. Enter Program Director's name: Robbie Hott, email: jh2jf@virginia.edu
4. On the "Intended Major/Minor" line, enter: **CS-BA** (for the new BACS)
5. You will be assigned a CS faculty advisor once your declaration is processed

For questions before declaring, email: bacsdirector@virginia.edu

**Important:** Engineering students cannot declare the BACS as a second major. Students from other schools (not Engineering or College) should contact bacsdirector@virginia.edu to check eligibility.
